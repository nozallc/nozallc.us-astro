Section 01 — Status (NOW)

What the current website is designed to do (Today)

Referral Support

Works best for people who already heard your name from a doctor or friend.

Trust & Legitimacy

Confirms you are a real and professional business to interested visitors.

Contact Center

Provides a reliable place for existing patients to find your phone and address.

Patient Searches

"Medication management Lexington KY" — People are actively searching for specific care right now.

Google Librarian

When pages try to answer too many questions, Google can't confidently recommend them.

Clinic in an Alley

Easy to recognize once found, but hard to discover if you don't already know it exists.

"A brochure-style website supports existing awareness — it does not create new demand."

What the site is NOT designed to do (Critical Gap)

Digital Brochure

A high-quality online version of a paper pamphlet showing basic services.

Current Structure

General pages group many services together, which makes it harder for Google to rank.

The Requirement

Google needs one specific page for one specific service in one specific city.

The Librarian

If a page covers too many topics, the Librarian (Google) won't know which question it answers best.

Market Result

Competitors with clearer, more focused pages often rank higher, even with smaller brands.

The Promoter

A modern site should act as a promoter that waves new people inside, not just opens the door.

"This website works like a doorman — not a promoter."

What Google & AI Engines See (Backend)

Limited Structure

One simple menu is trying to represent a very complex and multi-faceted practice.

Content Reality

Broad lists explain services to readers, but focused pages are what Google actually ranks.

Speed & Trust

The page loads, but not fast enough to feel instant. This affects patient patience and trust.

AI Limitation

New AI tools look for short, clear answers on focused pages — broad content leaves them confused.

Local Presence Gap

While locations appear in the footer, these signals are not strong enough for Google to rank effectively.

Platform Risk

Extensions act like patches that add functions but create a heavy, slow system that costs performance.

"The website is doing too much with too few pages."
